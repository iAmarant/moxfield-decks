##### **Presenting [[Ardenn, Intrepid Archaeologist]]**

Hello curious Planeswalker! I am Amarant and this is the Primer for one of my favourite decks: _Ardenn, Merchant of Cursed Equipment_. It's also my first Primer.

In this deck, humble entrepreneur [[Ardenn, Intrepid Archaeologist]] and his assistant [[Silas Renn, Seeker Adept]] look to equip allied and opposing creatures with their selection of carefully selected Auras and Equipment.

I feel that Ardenn is quite the popular Voltron commander. At the beginning of each combat during our turns, he lets us attach any number of equipment and auras we control to _any_ creature or player. We will be (ab)using the fact that we can target not only our creatures but also our opponent's creatures to create our own Super Soldier from our opponent's decks.

This is a _Group Voltron_ deck in which we beef up our own or preferably our opponents' best creatures with what I call _Cursed Loot and Blessings_.

##### **Theme: Cursed Loot and Blessings**

Cursed Loot are Equipment cards and Cursed Blessings are Aura cards, that offer a benefit for our opponent's creature and has either a drawback for it or a benefit for us.

- For example, [[Hot Soup]] benefits the equipped creature by making it unblockable, but any damage dealt to it will destroy the creature. Another example: [[Inquisitor's Flail]] has the creature deal double combat damage (good) and receive double combat damage (not good).

- Other types of Cursed Loot provide _us_ with a benefit: If we equip an opponent's creature with [[Sword of the Animist]], it will get stronger (+1/+1), and we, the controller of the sword, will fetch a basic land and put it into play each time that creature attacks.
- Our opponents will not be amused or try to cooperate with us when we begin equipping and enchanting their stuff, and will try to swing towards us whenever possible. This is why we also run several ways to goad our opponent's creatures into attacking our other opponents, my favorite being [[Bloodthirsty Blade]].

##### Theme: Give and Take

If you have seen the list, you might have noticed that I have chosen a ton of instant and sorceries that are very efficient but usually give something to our opponent as well. The classic examples here are [[Path To Exile]], [[Arcane Denial]], [[Generous Gift]] and [[An Offer You Can't Refuse]], but the deck has many more. I liked this idea of _Give and take_ and I tried to have most of my interactive spells be like this.

===panel: Power Level and Why Should You Play This

##### Commander Bracket: Upgraded (3)

This deck is made to fit best at the Upgraded Bracket (#3). With some luck and proper maneuvering I think it can hold its ground well against some Optimized(4) decks too. This is because we play a good amount of removal and some helpful Stax pieces like [[Torpor Orb]] and [[Authority of the Consuls]]. On the other hand, although we are not playing any Game Changers, I think that this deck would be a bit too strong for Core brackets.

##### Why Should You Play This Deck

The deck turns a conventional thing like Voltron on its head by focusing on making our opponent's creatures work for us. Give this deck a try if you want to play a different flavour of control where you are not just removing all threats, or constantly sweeping the board. Instead, you play a dangerous game of staying alive by enabling key creatures. You are secretly a James Bond villain.

##### Why Should You NOT Play This Deck

I think that this deck is quite demanding mentally because it asks us to keep track of the complete game state and to constantly evaluate our opponent's actions. This is caused due to the fact that we are playing many single-target removal spells.
Also, we have a somewhat passive game plan that depends heavily on what our opponents are playing.

===panel: Game Plan

[//]: # (Add a mulligan guide section here - what are you looking for in opening hands?)

Most of the time, we don't want to _control_ the game but to _manage_ it. What I mean by this, is that we don't want to remove all threats and strategies that our opponents play. What we want to be doing is removing key threats that would make one of our opponents run away with the game. At the same time, we use our array of goading cards to have our opponents beat each other with their best creatures. Eventually, as our opponents get eliminated, we retrieve all of our equipment and auras back into one of our creatures and swing for lethal.
===accordion
===panel: Early Game
The early game is very muted. We don't want to be missing any land drops and we want to play all the mana rocks we find on the way.

- Unless you get an early value Equipment like [[Sword of the Animist]], there's no need to play Ardenn early.
- Save your removal for the most impactful permanents that your opponents play early, like an [[Esper Sentinel]] or [[Rhystic Study]]. Also, don't fret if you get attacked on the early game, but you can choose to mitigate as much aggro as you are able to using your removal.
- We are running a [[Steelshaper's Gift]] and if we manage to draw it early, I would usually tutor for either [[Sword of the Animist]] or [[Bloodthirsty Blade]].

[//]: # (Consider adding specific threat assessment examples - what makes something "impactful"?)

===endpanel
===panel: Mid Game
At this point our opponents have begun to set up in full force and we will see one of them going forward. Try to goad the most resilient creature, or the one creature you don't want to be attacked with. This would often be their commander.
- On the midgame you should have some permanents you also want to attach to a creature that's not going to attack you, so you can be playing Ardenn. Don't be surprised if he dies before coming back to your turn. He's just a 2/2 with no abilities outside our turns.

===endpanel
===panel: Late Game
On the later stages of the game we have plenty of lands and some key equipment and enchantments already in play. At this point we maneuver patiently until all but one opponent is eliminated. We then get all of our Equipment back to our side with Ardenn (or paying mana) and swing for lethal. Either that or we use [[Reins of Power]] or [[Vorpal Sword]] to close up the game.

===panel: Removal

[//]: # (Good categorization, but consider adding how many total removal pieces and removal-to-threat ratio)

Our removal suite is quite extensive, I'm going to divide them into two: Permanent removal and Counterspells. Most of our single-target removal has the downside of providing the opponent with some value in exchange.
- For permament removal: We are running [[Path to Exile]], [[Pongify]], [[Reality Shift]], [[Murder]], [[Hero's Downfall]], [[Anguished Unmaking]], [[Despark]], [[Generous Gift]], [[Void Rend]], [[Grasp of Fate]] and [[Vindicate]]. We also have [[Heliod's Intervention]] and [[Canoptek Tomb Sentinel]] for artifacts and enchantments.
- Counterspells: [[Swan Song]], [[An Offer You Can't Refuse]], [[Arcane Denial]], [[Counterspell]], [[Dovin's Veto]], [[Force of Negation]].
- [[Mission Briefing]] help us get back a necessary instant or sorcery for a second cast.
- Finally, [[Captain's Hook]] offers the strangest type of removal where we have to pass it from creature to creature.
===endpanel
===panel: Goading and Politics

[//]: # (This section is somewhat redundant with explanations in other sections - consider consolidating)

- Cursed Loot and Blessings: [[Bloodthirsty Blade]] might be my favourite commander card and in this deck we utilise it to its full potential. Of course, we also include the Impetus cycle in [[Psychic Impetus]] and [[Ghoulish Impetus]].
- [[Redemption Arc]] is a surprisingly great aura which gives indestructible AND goading. The perfect card for the deck.
- [[Immortal Obligation]] is another card that shines in this deck. Firstly, the creature cannot attack or block us, so it doesn't affect us for combat math. Secondly, we can use it as a political tool by giving a blocker to an opponent, or using an Enter effect to deal with a troublesome permanent.
===endpanel
===panel: Protection
  In terms of protection, we are not running much. This is because we don't play many permanents that are must-protects in the deck.
- Nonetheless, Silas Renn, [[Academy Ruins]] and [[Buried Ruins]] help us get back any artifacts that our opponents destroy.
- [[Silent Arbiter]] is such a good card in the deck! Not only does it prevent us from getting suddenly alpha-striked by a horde of creatures, but due to the way goading works, our opponents _must_ attack with the goaded creatures first. This ensures that we're not the ones getting attacked.
- I also included [[Propaganda]] as another classic attack deterrent. [[Authority of the Consuls]] is here to stop all sort of haste shenanigans or flashing blockers, while also netting us some life on the side. It is a nonbo with [[Torpor Orb]], but alas.
- The aforementioned [[Torpor Orb]] helps us much more than hurt us by stopping all Enter effects. We are not really hurt by this as most of our triggers come by dealing combat damage or attacking.
- I have been trying [[Teferi, Mage of Zhalfir]] and it does a lot of work. It makes our spells almost uninteractable and stops cascade decks on their tracks. You just have to be careful because not all decks get hosed by it but those who do will come for you relentlessly!
===endpanel
===panel: Equips and Auras Explained

[//]: # (This section could use better organization - consider grouping by mana cost or priority)

- Attaching [[Assault Suit]] to an opponent's creature is probably one of the most hilarious effects of the deck: Due to Assault Suit's wording, we can exchange control of the equipped creature even if we don't control it! With it and any goading effect, we effectively create our own [[Alexios, Deimos of Kosmos]]. Soup the creature up with any of our auras and equipment that trigger on attacks and/or combat damage and benefit on each of your opponent's turns :)
- In order to make combat more _fun_ we are running [[Hot Soup]] and [[Inquisitor's Flail]].

We have some equipment that reward us for just attacking:

- [[Argentum Armor]] gives the equipped creature a nice P/T boost and we get to [[vindicate]] a permanent each time the creature attacks.
- As mentioned above, [[Sword of the Animist]] and [[Bitterthorn, Nissa's Animus]] let us search for lands.

[//]: # (This is redundant with the ramp section - consider removing or just referencing)

Other Equipment reward us when the equipped creature deals combat damage:
- [[Bloodforged Battle-Axe]] copies itself after each hit and we can equip all of them easily if Ardenn stays on the field.
- [[Zephyr Boots]], [[Mask of Riddles]], [[Sword of Fire and Ice]] and [[Sleeper's Robe]] provide both card draw for us and evasion for the creature.
- We use [[Sword of Feast and Famine]] mainly for its untapping ability, but giving the creature protection is also useful. Just be careful that we can't equip the creature with both [[Vorpal Sword]] and this one at the same time!
- Another neat piece of equipment in black is [[Wand of Orcus]]. No matter that we are not playing a Zombie subtheme, if we attach this to a big enough creature it won't be weird to be making 5+ Zombies with it.
- [[Quietus Spike]] and [[Vorpal Sword]] transform any creature into a must-kill for our opponents. The former takes away half their life if they can't block the attacking creature, while the latter can take their whole life if we have enough mana to activate its ability.

===panel: Finishers

- If our opponents' board still goes out of control and we don't die immediately, maybe thanks to Silent Arbiter or Propaganda, we can use [[Clone Legion]] to copy our opponent's board and move all of our Equipment to our side to finish the game.
- [[Reins of Power]] works in a very similar way. Because we are running very few creatures, this spell becomes a sort of 4-mana [[Insurrection]] at instant speed.
- We can realistically finish opponents using [[Vorpal Sword]]'s activated ability combined with any of our equipment that provide evasion, like [[Hot Soup]].

===panel: Lands
We're running a healthy 38 lands because we really don't want to be missing any land drops.

##### Colorless Lands

- [[Academy Ruins]] lets us retrieve any key equipment or mana rock that's destroyed by our opponents during the game.
- [[Arcane Lighthouse]] and [[Detection Tower]] let us get around that pesky Hexproof and Shroud that our opponent's creatures might have.
- Thanks to our card advantage engines, we will more often than not have a lot of cards in hand. That is the reason for the [[Reliquary Tower]].
- [[Scavenger Grounds]] acts as a one-off graveyard hate. We are not really affected too much by it because we almost don't use the graveyard. Don't activate it if you have [[Mission Briefing]] in hand or want to use Silas, though.

##### Monocolored Lands

- [[Castle Vantress]] and [[Rivendell]] help us scry unwanted cards away during the later turns or whenever we have mana to spare.
- [[Halimar Depths]] and [[Mystic Sanctuary]] help us recover some piece of interaction in the later turns.
- We play 6 [[Plains]], 5 [[Island]] and 3 [[Swamp]].

##### Two or More Colors

- [[Command Tower]] is a staple in any deck with 2+ colors.
We want to be able to produce Blue most of the time, followed by White and Black only for a few permanents or in the late game to activate Vorpal Sword.
- For lands that tap for White-Blue we are running [[Glacial Fortress]], [[Hallowed Fountain]], [[Irrigated Farmland]], [[Mystic Gate]], [[Nimbus Maze]], [[Port Town]], [[Prairie Stream]] and [[Temple of Enlightenment]].
- For lands that tap for Blue-Black we have [[Drowned Catacomb]], [[Watery Grave]], [[Sunken Hollow]] and [[Temple of Deceit]].
